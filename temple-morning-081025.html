Upanayanam Schedule

Date: 10-Oct-25

The upanayanam marks the sacred initiation of the groom. During this ceremony he receives the sacred thread (yajnopavita) and is symbolically admitted into Vedic learning and ritual life. It signifies purity, discipline, and readiness to take on spiritual and household responsibilities.

  • 08:30 — Upanayanam begins in Small Yagashala: Groom is sat by the holy fire and rituals begin.
  • 09:30 — Kumara Bhojanam: Mother and sisters of the groom along with the groom and Brahmachari's (Upanayanam done but not yet married) in the groom’s family are served a special and auspicious meal. This act symbolizes prosperity, the blessing of future generations, and continuity of lineage in the family.
  • 11:14 — Muhurtham: Auspicious time for the Upanayanam where the groom wears a sacred thread across his body and symbolic rituals like ear piercing and hair cutting etc. are performed.
  • Snatakam: This is a “graduation” rite where the groom symbolically completes his student life and prepares to enter the stage of a householder. It signifies the groom’s readiness to take on responsibilities of family and society.
  • Kashi Yatra: The groom pretends to renounce all worldly pleasures and sets off on a mock pilgrimage to Kashi to become an ascetic. Carrying a walking stick, an umbrella, and other items, he starts walking away. The bride's brother then intercepts him and convinces him to return and accept married life. The rituals conclude with Lunch at 1:00 pm.
