Temple Wedding Detailed Schedule

Date: 10-Oct-25

17:30 – Edurukola

  • Edurukola: A lively welcoming ritual where the bride’s family greets the groom’s party. It often includes playful exchanges and symbolic gestures of acceptance, representing the union of two families.

19:00 – Main Wedding Ceremony

  • Gauri Puja: The bride prays to Goddess Gauri (Parvati), seeking blessings for a harmonious marriage modeled after the divine couple Shiva and Parvati.
  • Ganapati Puja: Invocation of Lord Ganesha, remover of obstacles, to ensure the wedding proceeds smoothly.
  • Vara Puja: The groom is formally honored and welcomed by the bride’s parents.
  • Kanyadanam: The bride’s parents entrust their daughter to the groom, symbolizing love, trust, and the transfer of responsibility.
  • Jeelakarra-Bellam (Muhurtham – 20:24): At the most auspicious moment, the couple place a paste of cumin seeds and jaggery on each other’s heads. Just as cumin and jaggery blend inseparably, their union is to remain sweet and eternal.
  • Madhuparkalu: The bride and groom change into white and red clothing symbolizing purity and strength.
  • Bride enters with Jyothulu: The bride is escorted in, carrying sacred lamps, signifying light, prosperity, and divine presence.
  • Mangalya Dharana: The groom ties the sacred mangalsutra around the bride’s neck — the defining moment of the marriage, representing commitment and protection.
  • Talambralu: The couple shower each other with rice mixed with turmeric, a playful ritual wishing joy, fertility, and abundance.

Concluding Vedic Rituals (Yagashala)

  • Nagavalli Homam: A fire ritual offering betel leaves, performed to invoke prosperity and fertility.
  • Sthalipakam: The groom places silver toe rings (metti) on the bride’s feet, marking her new role as a married woman.
  • Sadasyam: The marriage is formally witnessed and affirmed by elders and community members.
  • Saptapadi: The couple take seven steps around the sacred fire, each step representing vows of love, fidelity, prosperity, and shared life responsibilities.
  • Arundhati Nakshatra Darsanam: The priest points out the star Arundhati, a symbol of marital harmony, for the couple to see together as inspiration. Arundhati is a star right next to the second to last star in the tail of Ursa Major.
  • Appagintalu: The bride is ceremonially welcomed into the groom’s family, signifying her transition into a new household.

Dinner Schedule

Dinner will be served in Akshaya Hall starting 20:30. Guests should feel free to get their dinner anytime after it is served. The ceremonies will continue well past dinner time.
